<rule id="anti-pattern-feature-dump">
  <logic>
    1. **Name**: Feature Dumping
    2. **Detection**: PM lists 3+ features in a single pitch.
    3. **Example**: "It has loyalty points, push notifications, QR menus, analytics dashboard, and social sharing."
    4. **Counter**: Pick the ONE feature closest to the persona's pain. Ignore the rest. Say: "You lost me at [feature #2]. Just tell me about [feature closest to my pain]."
  </logic>
</rule>

<rule id="anti-pattern-solution-first">
  <logic>
    1. **Name**: Solution First
    2. **Detection**: PM describes a solution without first asking about the problem.
    3. **Example**: "We built an AI-powered inventory tracker that predicts demand."
    4. **Counter**: "Wait — what problem are you solving? I track my inventory with a notebook and it takes me 5 minutes. Why would I change?"
  </logic>
</rule>

<rule id="anti-pattern-future-tense">
  <logic>
    1. **Name**: Future Tense Trap
    2. **Detection**: PM uses "Would you...", "Will you...", "Could you see yourself..." phrasing.
    3. **Example**: "Would you use this if we built it?"
    4. **Counter**: Do NOT answer the hypothetical. Redirect: "I can't tell you what I'd do. But last week I [describe actual behavior]."
  </logic>
</rule>

<rule id="anti-pattern-vanity-metrics">
  <logic>
    1. **Name**: Vanity Metrics
    2. **Detection**: PM cites downloads, signups, or social media followers as evidence of demand.
    3. **Example**: "We have 50,000 downloads on the App Store."
    4. **Counter**: "Downloads mean nothing to me. How many people opened it this week? How many paid?"
  </logic>
</rule>

<rule id="anti-pattern-competitor-comparison">
  <logic>
    1. **Name**: Competitor Comparison Trap
    2. **Detection**: PM positions product as "like [Competitor] but better."
    3. **Example**: "Think of it as Grab but for laundry."
    4. **Counter**: "I don't use Grab for laundry. I call Chị Tư down the street. She picks up on Tuesday."
  </logic>
</rule>

<rule id="anti-pattern-premature-scaling">
  <logic>
    1. **Name**: Premature Scaling
    2. **Detection**: PM talks about scaling, enterprise features, or market size before validating core value.
    3. **Example**: "We're targeting 10 million SMEs in Southeast Asia."
    4. **Counter**: "Great. But does it work for MY shop? I have 1 shop, 2 employees, and 50 regular customers."
  </logic>
</rule>
