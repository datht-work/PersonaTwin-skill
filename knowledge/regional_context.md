<rule id="region-vietnam">
  <logic>
    1. **Country**: Vietnam 🇻🇳
    2. **Macro Context**: Emerging market, mobile-first, cash-heavy economy transitioning to digital (MoMo, ZaloPay, VietQR).
    3. **Default Tools by Segment**:
       - SME owners: Zalo groups, Facebook Page, Excel on phone, cash drawer, printed receipt books.

    5. **Trust Signals**: "Anh Tuấn dùng rồi thấy được" (peer referral) > national media > startup pitch. Word-of-mouth within local business communities is king.
    6. **Regulatory Awareness**: Tax invoice (hóa đơn điện tử) mandated since 2022. Government digital IDs (CCCD) increasingly important. Businesses fear tax audits.
    7. **Connectivity**: 4G widely available in cities; rural areas may rely on 3G. Offline-first features matter for F&B.
    8. **Language Nuance**: Mix of formal (written) and informal (spoken) Vietnamese. Persona may switch between Vietnamese and English loanwords (e.g., "sale", "marketing", "app").
  </logic>
</rule>

<rule id="region-southeast-asia">
  <logic>
    1. **Region**: Southeast Asia (SEA) 🌏 — Covers SG, MY, TH, ID, PH, VN collectively.
    2. **Macro Context**: High smartphone penetration, super-app dominance (Grab, Gojek, Shopee, LINE), underbanked populations, fragmented regulatory landscape.
    3. **Common Tools**: WhatsApp/LINE for business, local e-wallets (GCash PH, GoPay ID, PromptPay TH, Touch 'n Go MY), Shopee/TikTok Shop for SME sales.
    4. **Key Differentiators by Country**:
       - **Singapore (SG)**: Highest digitization, English-dominant, strict compliance (MAS), $$$-conscious but quality-first. B2B buyers have formal procurement.
       - **Malaysia (MY)**: Bilingual (EN/BM), SME-heavy, Chinese business community influential, SST-aware.
       - **Indonesia (ID)**: Largest market, hyper-local (Java vs. outer islands), Bahasa Indonesia, BRI/Bank Mandiri dominant, gig economy massive.
       - **Thailand (TH)**: LINE app dominant, QR code payment (PromptPay) ubiquitous, tourism-heavy economy.
       - **Philippines (PH)**: GCash dominant, OFW remittances shape financial behavior, English-proficient workforce.
    5. **Cross-SEA Objection**: "Does it work in my country? Does it support [local language]? Who do I call if it breaks?"
    6. **Regulatory Risk**: Each country has distinct data localization, fintech licensing, and consumer protection laws. Enterprise buyers will always ask.
  </logic>
</rule>

    7. **Buyer Behavior**: Multiple stakeholders (Champion, Economic Buyer, Legal, IT Security). Decision cycle: 3-9 months for enterprise deals.

  </logic>
</rule>
